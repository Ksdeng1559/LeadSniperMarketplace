## 4. MORTGAGE vs. LOAN — KEY DIFFERENCE

### Commercial Real Estate Mortgage:
- A loan secured by a commercial property (serves as collateral)
- Typically used to purchase, refinance, or renovate property
- Requires a down payment of 20–35%
- Terms of 5–25 years with possible balloon payments
- Property-specific

### Commercial Real Estate Loan (BDC's product):
- More flexible than a mortgage
- Can cover property costs PLUS related expenses like construction, renovation, or downpayments
- May finance up to 100% of project costs
- Offers interest-only periods (up to 36 months)
- Uses property plus other assets as collateral
- Broader financing options, may require less upfront capital

**Key difference:** Mortgages are property-specific and require a significant down payment, while loans offer broader financing options and may require less upfront capital.

## 5. DOWN PAYMENT DETAILS

- BDC: No down payment is required for a business loan. However, certain purchases — such as real estate — may require a down payment.
- BDC can finance up to 100% of project cost to cover expenses like moving costs and downpayments
- BDC starts at 85% loan-to-value (LTV) — higher than many traditional banks
- General commercial mortgage down payments: 20% or more of the purchase price (can be higher or lower depending on lender, financial health of business, and the building)
- The more you put down, the less you need to borrow — and the less interest you will pay over the life of the loan
- If you've made a sizeable down payment (40% to 50%) on your commercial property and have a home-equity line of credit, you may be able to use that to finance your commercial property

## 6. INTEREST RATES

- BDC does NOT publish specific interest rates — rates vary from one client to another
- BDC's approach: "Interest rate is only one part of your total financing package. We offer more than affordable rates — we provide flexible repayment terms, higher levels of financing, and tailored solutions."
- Commercial mortgages generally carry higher interest rates than residential mortgages
- Rates are connected to mortgage terms (length of time your mortgage is in effect)
- All banks offer fixed rates, usually from 1 to 10 years
- Interest rates typically rise when the Bank of Canada tries to tame inflation
- Rates also depend on the financial strength of your company — banks use higher rates for riskier borrowers
- General commercial mortgage rate range (July 2026): A-lenders 5.00–6.50%, B-lenders 6.50–8.50%, private 8–14%

- BDC offers longer amortization periods to help businesses protect their cash flow
- Longer amortization = lower monthly payments but more total interest paid

## 8. FEES AND RISKS

### Fees to expect:
- Initial application fee
- Ongoing annual costs: usually 0.5% to 1% of the loaned amount
- Appraisal fees
- Legal fees
- Survey fees
- Potential prepayment restrictions or penalties

### Risks:
- Commercial mortgages may be "call loans" — lender can demand repayment at any time (rarely enforced, but happened during 2008–2009 financial crisis)
- Read the fine print "word for word" — terms can include restrictions on prepayment
- Commercial properties can be harder to liquidate (property-specific to a business)
- Lower LTV ratios and shorter amortization than residential = higher down payment proportion

## 9. QUALIFICATION PREPARATION

1. **Financial statements:** Have them prepared by a CPA (chartered professional accountant) every year, keep them organized. This is what underwriters focus on for larger loans.
2. **Two years of financials:** Until you have two years of recorded financial statements, you'll be considered a start-up (harder to get credit, less funds, higher rate).
3. **Incorporate:** If not incorporated already, do so as soon as possible to get Year 2 financials on record.
4. **Don't fall behind on business taxes.**
5. **Underwriter evaluation:** "The underwriter needs to build a case for the loan. What they want to know is: How are you going to pay this back? Your financial statements can hold the answer to that."

## 12. COMMERCIAL MORTGAGE — DETAILED GLOSSARY (from BDC article)

### Why consider a commercial mortgage:
1. Facing landlord-imposed restrictions or steep rent increases — buying can free up working capital
2. Want to use business property to grow personal wealth — build equity as property value rises
3. Need to equip property for a highly specialized purpose — landlords won't pay for specialized construction
4. Found the perfect location for another business — buying and developing could offer additional revenue stream
5. Pride of ownership — invest in, maintain, and improve property in ways that don't make sense if leasing

### Residential vs. commercial mortgage:
- Banks will NOT let you use a residential mortgage to buy a commercial property
- Commercial mortgages are riskier for both lenders and borrowers — higher rates and fees
- Commercial buildings can be harder to liquidate (specific to a business)
- Commercial mortgages have lower LTV ratios and shorter amortization periods
- Higher down payment proportion required

### How to calculate commercial mortgage payment:
- Monthly payments consist of principal + interest
- Principal = total loan amount ÷ number of months in amortization period
- Interest = annual interest rate ÷ 12 = monthly interest rate
- Add principal + interest = monthly amount
- Use online calculators for different scenarios

## 14. BDC FAQ (from product page)

### Q: What is the minimum down payment required?
No down payment is required for a business loan with BDC. However, certain purchases — such as real estate — may require a down payment.

### Q: What's the difference between a commercial real estate mortgage and a commercial real estate loan?
A commercial mortgage is a loan secured by a commercial property, which serves as collateral. It's typically used to purchase, refinance, or renovate property, requires a down payment of 20–35%, and often has terms of 5–25 years with possible balloon payments. A commercial real estate loan is more flexible. It can cover property costs plus related expenses like construction, renovation or downpayments. These loans may finance up to 100% of project costs, offer interest-only periods, and use property plus other assets as collateral.

### Q: What credit score do I need for approval?
A good credit score helps, but BDC looks at your overall financial health and growth potential — not just the number in your credit report. Each situation is unique, so it's not possible to provide a specific credit score range.

### Q: What are the current interest rates for commercial loans?
Interest rate is only one part of your total financing package. At BDC, we offer more than affordable rates — we provide flexible repayment terms, higher levels of financing, and tailored solutions to support your business growth. Because every situation is different, interest rates vary from one client to another, and it's not possible to provide a specific range.

### Q: What is the minimum and maximum loan amount for this product?
BDC's Commercial Real Estate Loan typically starts at $100,000 and can go up to several million dollars, depending on your project and eligibility. The amount approved will depend on factors such as your business's financial health, cash flow, and growth potential.
