## 1. PROGRAM OVERVIEW

**Program Name:** Canada Small Business Financing Program (CSBFP)
**Administered by:** Small Business Financing Directorate, Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada (ISED)
**Legislation:** Canada Small Business Financing Act + Canada Small Business Financing Regulations
**Date of last regulatory amendment:** July 4, 2022
**Program website:** canada.ca/csbfp
**Toll-free info line:** 1-866-959-1699
**Email:** csbfp-pfpec@ised-isde.gc.ca

**Description:**
The CSBFP makes it easier for small businesses to get loans from financial institutions by sharing the risk with lenders. The government guarantees a portion of the loan, allowing lenders to approve financing they might otherwise decline. The enhanced program (post-July 2022) provides additional financing products, a new class of loans (line of credit), increased loan amounts and terms, improved loan conditions, and decreased administrative burden.

**Historical stats:** Over the past 10 years, small businesses have received over 53,000 CSBFP loans totalling more than $11 billion.

## 2. ELIGIBILITY

### Who is eligible:
- Most start-ups and existing **for-profit**, not-for-profit, and charitable small businesses in Canada
- Gross annual revenues of **$10 million or less**
- Business structures: corporations, sole proprietors, partnerships, or cooperatives
- Must be operating in Canada

### Who is NOT eligible:
- **Farming businesses** (use the Canadian Agricultural Loans Act program instead)
- Share purchases
- Assets that a holding company acquires

### Business acquisition:
The purchase of eligible assets of an existing business may qualify for CSBFP financing. You may finance the lesser of the cost of purchase and the appraised value of the eligible assets. Contact your financial institution before committing to appraisal expenses.

## 3. LOAN LIMITS (post-July 2022 amendments)

### Maximum total loan amount: $1,150,000 per borrower

**Term loans:**
- Maximum: **$1,000,000** for term loans
- Of which maximum **$500,000** for equipment and leasehold improvements (up from $350,000 pre-2022)
- Of which maximum **$150,000** for intangible assets and working capital costs

**Lines of credit (NEW since July 2022):**
- Maximum: **$150,000** for lines of credit
- This is OVER AND ABOVE the $150,000 working capital available under the term loan product
- For working capital costs only (day-to-day operating expenses)

### Maximum loan term (government coverage period):
- Term loans: **15 years** (increased from previous limits)
- Lines of credit: **5 years** (renewable)

### Line of credit renewal options (before end of 5-year term):
1. Re-register for a new 5-year period (new registration form + 2% fee required)
2. Convert to a CSBFP term loan (max 10-year coverage, rate ≤ prime + 5%)
3. Repay balance with a conventional loan

## 4. WHAT CAN BE FINANCED

### Term loans can finance:
- Purchase or improvement of land and buildings used for commercial purposes
- Purchase or improvement of new or used equipment (including commercial vehicles)
- Purchase of new or existing leasehold improvements (renovations to leased property)
- Intangible assets (franchise fees, goodwill, incorporation costs, permits, licenses, capitalized R&D)
- Working capital costs (inventory, software/websites, printed materials, professional fees, R&D, payroll, rent)
- The registration fee

### Lines of credit can finance:
- Working capital costs (day-to-day operating expenses)
- The registration fee

### Examples of term loan uses:
- Commercial vehicles
- Hotel or restaurant equipment
- Computer or telecommunications equipment and software
- Production equipment
- Costs to buy a franchise

### Intangible assets (definition):
A non-monetary asset without physical substance that can be sold, transferred, licensed, rented, or exchanged, or that arises from a contractual or other legal right. Examples: franchise fees, goodwill, incorporation costs, permits and licenses used in the operation of eligible assets, capitalized research/development costs.

### Working capital costs (definition):
Costs to fund the day-to-day operating expenses of a business. Examples: inventory, expenses related to the creation and development of software and websites, printed materials (brochures, flyers, business cards, menus, photocopies), professional fees (e.g., legal, accounting, appraisal), research and development costs, payroll, rent.

### What is NOT eligible:
- Share purchases
- Assets that a holding company acquires
- (List is not exhaustive — check with lender)

## 5. INTEREST RATES AND FEES

### Term loan interest rates (set by lender, capped by program):
- **Floating rate:** Maximum = lender's prime lending rate + **3%**
- **Fixed rate:** Maximum = lender's single family residential mortgage rate + **3%**

### Line of credit interest rate:
- **Maximum = lender's prime lending rate + 5%**

### Registration fee:
- **2%** of the total amount loaned (term loans) or total amount authorized (lines of credit)
- Can be financed as part of the loan
- Must be paid by borrower to lender

### Annual administration fee:
- **1.25%** applied to end-of-month loan balances (term loans)
- **1.25%** on the daily outstanding amount or average daily balance for each month (lines of credit)

### Lender fees:
- Lenders may charge same fees as conventional loans (set-up, renewal fees)
- These fees are paid directly to the lender and CANNOT be financed under the program

---

## 6. SECURITY REQUIREMENTS

### Term loans:
- **Real property and equipment:** Lender must take security on the assets financed
- **Leasehold improvements, software, intangible assets, working capital costs:** Lender must take security on any business assets (e.g., General Security Agreement)

### Lines of credit:
- Lender must take security on any assets of the small business for the authorized amount (e.g., General Security Agreement)

### Personal guarantee:
- Lenders have the option to take an **unsecured personal guarantee**
- A lender may release a guarantor if the loan is in good standing (the 50% principal repayment condition has been removed)

## 7. APPLICATION PROCESS

1. **Present your business plan** directly to any bank, credit union, or caisse populaire in Canada
2. The financial officer reviews your business proposal
3. The financial institution makes the approval decision (ISED does not get involved)
4. If approved, the financial institution disburses the funds
5. The financial institution registers the loan with ISED
6. Find a lender: https://www.ised-isde.canada.ca/site/maps/en/finance-map

**Key point:** Financial institutions deliver the program and are solely responsible for approving the loan. The money the borrower receives is from the financial institution, not the government. ISED does not accept or process loan applications.

### Eligible expenditures time period:
- Expenditures or commitments can be financed if they arise no more than **365 days** prior to the date the term loan is approved (increased from 180 days pre-2022)
- For lines of credit: expenditures must arise or be invoiced no more than **365 days** prior to the date the line of credit was authorized

### Registration period:
- Term loans: Must be registered within **6 months** from the date of first loan disbursement (increased from 3 months)
- Lines of credit: Must be registered within **6 months** from the date the line of credit is opened

## 8. DEFAULT AND CLAIMS

### Default process:
- Default occurs when borrower fails to comply with a material term of the loan/LOC agreement (e.g., failure to make payment)
- Lender provides borrower with a demand for repayment within a specified period (no longer requires separate notice of default)
- Claim must be submitted within **60 months** after the day on which the last payment is received

### Claim documentation:
- Term loans: Documentation substantiating cost and proof of payment reduced from 100% to **75%** of principal amount outstanding
- Lines of credit: No documentation of cost/proof of payment required. Instead, lender submits an attestation form signed by borrower stating:
  - The LOC is for working capital costs of day-to-day operational expenses
  - Expenses did not arise/were not invoiced more than 365 days before the LOC was authorized

### CSBFP liability limit for lenders:
- Lines of credit liability: **15%** of total authorized and registered LOCs by that lender
- This is separate from the lender's term loan liability calculation
- Line of credit liability commenced in the C5 lending period (ended March 31, 2024)

## 9. PARTICIPATING LENDERS

Most major Canadian financial institutions participate:
- Banks (RBC, TD, BMO, CIBC, Scotiabank, National Bank, etc.)
- Credit unions
- Caisses populaires
- Other financial institutions

**Note:** Not all financial institutions currently offer the line of credit product (as of 2026). Many are still rolling it out. Check with your specific institution.

**RBC specific:** Offers a "CSBF Line of Credit" — a 2-way revolving line of credit that automatically deposits funds into operating account when low.

Find a lender: https://www.ised-isde.canada.ca/site/maps/en/finance-map

## 10. FAQ — OFFICIAL GOVERNMENT ANSWERS

### Q1: What businesses are eligible to apply under the CSBFP?
Most start-ups and existing for-profit, not-for-profit and charitable small businesses in Canada with gross revenues of $10 million or less are eligible. Businesses can be corporations, sole proprietors, partnerships or cooperatives. Farming businesses are not eligible.

### Q2: What is eligible for financing under the CSBFP?
Term loans can finance: purchase/improvement of real property for commercial use, new/used equipment, leasehold improvements, intangible assets, working capital costs, and the registration fee. Lines of credit can finance: working capital costs and the registration fee.

### Q3: Can I purchase an existing business under the CSBFP?
The purchase of eligible assets of an existing business may qualify. You may finance the lesser of the cost of purchase and the appraised value of the eligible assets.

### Q4: What is not eligible for financing?
Share purchases or assets that a holding company acquires. The list is not exhaustive.

### Q5: How much can be financed and what is the maximum loan amount?
Maximum is $1.15 million, which includes $1 million for term loans and $150,000 for lines of credit.

### Q6: What are the costs associated with a CSBFP loan?
- Registration fee: 2% of total loan amount (can be financed)
- Interest rate (term loans): max prime + 3% (floating) or residential mortgage rate + 3% (fixed)
- Interest rate (lines of credit): max prime + 5%
- Annual administration fee: 1.25% on end-of-month balances
- Lender fees: same as conventional loans, cannot be financed

### Q7: Where can I apply for a CSBFP loan?
Banks, credit unions, caisses populaires and other financial institutions. Present your business proposal directly to a financial institution. They are solely responsible for approval decisions.

### Q8: Do I need to communicate with ISED for a loan?
No. The program is delivered by financial institutions. ISED provides program information but does not get involved in approval decisions or accept/process applications.

### Q9: Why do some financial institutions choose not to participate?
The majority participate. The decision is up to each individual institution.

### Q10: Why is my financial institution not currently offering the line of credit product?
Only some institutions currently offer it as it takes time to implement. Many are still rolling it out.

### Q11: What if my application is rejected?
Contact another financial institution (different criteria). Or visit Canada's Business Benefits Finder for other government funding options.

### Q12: What if I'm being treated unfairly by my financial institution?
1. Talk with your financial officer
2. Escalate to branch/district/regional manager
3. Contact the Ombudsman for Banking Services and Investments (obsi.ca)

### Q13: Where can I go for additional information if I can't access CSBFP financing?
Canada's Business Benefits Finder (innovation.canada.ca) for other government grants, loans, and financing options.
