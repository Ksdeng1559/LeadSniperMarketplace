Frequently Asked Questions

What is a business line of credit in Canada?
A business line of credit is a revolving credit facility that lets you borrow up to a pre-set limit, repay, and borrow again. You only pay interest on the amount you use, not the full limit. In Canada, lines of credit are available from major banks, credit unions, and alternative lenders, with limits ranging from $5,000 to $3 million depending on your business profile and collateral.
Can I get a line of credit on a business account in Canada?
Yes. Canadian banks and credit unions offer business lines of credit on business accounts. You can apply as a sole proprietor, partnership, or corporation. Most lenders require bank statements, financial statements, and proof of business operations. The CSBFP also offers government-backed lines of credit up to $150,000 for eligible small businesses.
How much interest is on a $100,000 line of credit?
Interest on a $100,000 business line of credit depends on the rate. At 8% effective, you would pay approximately $667/month in interest if you carry the full $100,000 balance. You only pay interest on the drawn amount, not the credit limit. Rates typically range from 5.95% to 16.35% depending on whether the line is secured or unsecured.
What credit score do I need for a business line of credit in Canada?
Most Canadian banks require a personal credit score of 680+ for a business line of credit. Alternative lenders may accept scores as low as 500, but with higher interest rates. The CSBFP-backed line of credit has more flexible requirements since the government guarantees a portion of the loan.

What Determines Your Commercial Mortgage Rate

Lenders price commercial mortgages on risk. Four factors move your rate more than anything else.

📊

Debt Service Coverage (DSCR)

The property's net operating income divided by annual debt payments. A DSCR of 1.40x+ earns better pricing than the 1.20x minimum — more cushion means less lender risk.

🏷️

Loan-to-Value (LTV)

Lower LTV, lower rate. Financing 55% of the property value prices meaningfully better than the 75–80% maximum. Every 5–10% of extra equity typically shaves basis points.

🏢

Property Type & Tenancy

Multi-family and industrial price tightest. Hospitality, single-tenant retail, and special-purpose properties carry premiums of 50–150 bps due to income volatility.

📅

Term & Insurance

CMHC insurance buys the lowest rates in the market. Shorter terms and variable rates track prime (currently 4.45%); 5-year fixed terms track bond yields plus a spread.

How the Prime Rate Affects Commercial Mortgage Rates

Variable rates

Priced as prime plus a spread (e.g., prime + 1.00–2.50%). When the Bank of Canada moves its policy rate, variable commercial mortgage payments move with it — usually within days.

Fixed rates

Priced off Government of Canada bond yields plus a lender spread, not prime. Fixed rates can fall or rise ahead of BoC announcements as bond markets price in expectations.

Current environment

Prime sits at 4.45% (July 2026). CMHC-insured multi-family remains the cheapest commercial money in Canada, with conventional bank pricing roughly 75–225 bps above insured rates.

What is the interest rate on a commercial mortgage in Canada?
As of July 2026, commercial mortgage rates in Canada range from 4.25% to 8.50%. CMHC-insured multi-family rates start at 4.25%–5.25%. Conventional A-lender rates range from 5.00% to 6.50%. B-lender financing ranges from 6.50% to 8.50%. The prime rate is currently 4.45%.
What is the lowest interest rate for commercial property in Canada?
The lowest commercial mortgage rates are CMHC-insured multi-family residential properties, starting at 4.25%. These rates are available for properties with 5+ residential units where the borrower qualifies for CMHC insurance.
Are commercial mortgage rates higher than residential rates in Canada?
Yes, typically by 1%–2%. Commercial properties carry more income risk than owner-occupied homes, so lenders price in a premium. The exception is CMHC-insured multi-family (5+ units), where rates from 4.25% can rival or beat residential pricing because the government insures the lender's risk.
Should I choose a fixed or variable commercial mortgage rate?
Variable rates track prime (currently 4.45%) plus a spread and adjust when the Bank of Canada moves. Fixed rates are priced off bond yields and lock your payment for the term. Variable suits borrowers who can absorb payment swings; fixed suits tight DSCR situations where payment certainty protects your covenant. Many commercial borrowers split the difference with shorter fixed terms (3–5 years).

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I finance a business acquisition in Canada?
Business acquisitions in Canada are typically financed through a combination of: (1) Senior debt — bank loans secured by the target company's assets, (2) Government-backed CSBFP loans up to $1,000,000, (3) Seller financing (vendor take-back), (4) Mezzanine or cash flow loans from alternative lenders like BDC, and (5) Buyer's personal equity (typically 10%+ down payment).
How much down payment do I need to buy a business in Canada?
Most Canadian lenders require a personal down payment of at least 10% of the purchase price. The remaining 90% can be financed through a combination of senior debt, CSBFP loans, and seller financing. Stronger deals may require less personal cash, while riskier acquisitions may need 20–30% down.
What is seller financing (vendor take-back)?
Seller financing, also known as a vendor take-back or vendor note, is when the previous owner agrees to finance a portion of the purchase price. The buyer pays the seller over time instead of paying the full amount upfront. Typical vendor take-backs cover 10–40% of the purchase price.
How do lenders value a business for acquisition financing?
Lenders evaluate acquisition targets using a multiple of EBITDA (Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization). Typical multiples range from 2x–5x EBITDA for small businesses, depending on industry, growth, and stability.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Canada Small Business Financing Program (CSBFP)?
The CSBFP is a federal program that makes it easier for small businesses to get loans from financial institutions by sharing the risk with lenders. The government guarantees a portion of the loan, allowing lenders to approve financing they might otherwise decline. As of 2026, the program offers term loans up to $1,000,000 and lines of credit up to $150,000.
How much can I borrow under the CSBFP?
The maximum CSBFP loan is $1,000,000 for term loans, of which no more than $500,000 can be used for leasehold improvements and equipment. Lines of credit are available up to $150,000.
Who is eligible for the CSBFP?
Your business must: (1) operate in Canada, (2) have gross annual revenues of $10 million or less, (3) be a for-profit business, and (4) use the loan for an eligible purpose. Startups and existing businesses are both eligible.
What are the CSBFP interest rates?
CSBFP rates are set by the lender but capped at prime + 3%. As of July 2026, with prime at 4.45%, typical rates range from 6.20% to 7.45%.

Documents You'll Need to Apply

Commercial mortgage underwriting is document-heavy. Having these ready cuts weeks off approval.

  • Business financial statements — 2–3 years, accountant-prepared (Notice to Reader minimum; Review Engagement preferred for larger loans)
  • Personal net worth statement — assets, liabilities, and contingent guarantees for each principal owner
  • Property appraisal — from an AACI-designated appraiser; lender typically orders it, borrower pays
  • Rent roll and leases — current tenancy schedule with lease terms, renewal options, and arrears (income properties)
  • Purchase agreement or refinance statement — accepted offer, or current mortgage statement if refinancing
  • Environmental assessment — Phase I ESA commonly required for industrial, automotive, and fuel-adjacent properties
  • Business plan or property pro forma — projected income and expenses, especially for owner-occupied or repositioning deals

What is a commercial mortgage in Canada?
A commercial mortgage is a loan secured by commercial real estate — office buildings, retail spaces, industrial properties, multi-family residential (5+ units), or mixed-use buildings. Unlike residential mortgages, commercial mortgages are underwritten based on the property's income-generating potential (DSCR) in addition to the borrower's credit.
How much down payment do I need for a commercial mortgage in Canada?
Commercial mortgage down payments in Canada typically range from 15% to 35%. A-lenders usually require 25–35% down. B-lenders may accept 15–25%. CMHC-insured multi-family properties can have down payments as low as 20%.
What are the requirements for a commercial mortgage in Canada?
Requirements include: 15–35% down payment, DSCR of 1.15x or higher, property appraisal, business financial statements (2–3 years), personal credit score of 680+ (A-lenders), and proof of rental income or business cash flow.
Can I get a commercial mortgage from a private lender in Canada?
Yes. Private commercial mortgage lenders in Canada offer financing when traditional banks decline. They focus on property equity rather than borrower credit, with rates typically 8%–14% and terms of 1–3 years. Approval can be as fast as 5–7 days.
